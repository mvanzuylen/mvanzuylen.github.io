Truffle Acorn Squash Soup

This is my favorite fall/winter soup! It's vegetarian, and my friends have given it rave reviews, including the ones that don't like truffle.

Ingredients

  • 1 large acorn squash
  • 5 tablespoons truffle oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 large yellow onion
  • 2 medium carrots
  • 4 tablespoons minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1-2 cups water
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon sage
  • dash of nutmeg
  • 1.5 cups pecorino
  • salt and pepper

Special Tools

  • Imersion Blender

Method

  • Cut the acorn squash in half and rub 2 tablespoons of truffle oil on the squash.
  • Poke holes in the squash with a fork to let the truffle oil sink in.
  • Roast the squash at 400F until bubbling and starting to turn brown (~50min).
  • Remove the squash flesh from the skin and set the squash flesh aside to cool.
  • Use remaining tuffle oil and butter to soften onion and carrots and starting to brown and stick to the botton of the pan (~10min).
  • Scrape off the brown buts and add garlic, honey, and some salt and pepper.
  • Add in the acorn squash.
  • Add in the veggie broth, sage, nutmeg, and bay leaf.
  • Cover and simmer for 10-20min.
  • Use the imersion blender to blend until smooth.
  • Add in 1-2 cups water to make the soup as thin as you want.
  • Add in the pecorino and stir until melted.
  • Add more salt and truffle oil as needed.
